Hello!

My name is Giulia and I created this website to tell something (I hope) interesting about myself which could support my application to Founders&Coders.

I am an Italian living in London, a philosphy graduate and charity worker with a passion for music, politics and foreign languages.

I've always been an extremely curious person, eager to learn new things and challenge myself.

After thinking for years that coding was 'too difficult' for me, last summer I decided to give it a try and I discovered that I love it!

In the future I dream to become a skilled software developer to build websites and apps that have a positive impact on society.

For now, I am having fun learning and experimenting with Javascript on freeCodeCamp, Codewars and Github.

How
Languages
Changed My Life

"The limits of my language mean the limits of my world." (Ludwig Wittgenstein)

In my life I've had the chance to learn six different languages, each of which opened up new worlds to me and offered me new ways of thinking. This website is dedicated to these languages and how they enriched and changed my life.

Italian

Italian is the language I grew up with, and which I am proud of speaking. It is a cheerful, melodic and colourful language, full of funny expressions and ways of saying derived from the many Italian dialects and always accompanied by dozens of hand gestures. It is a language that always instantly feel more outgoing and emotional, ready to enjoy la dolce vita.

Italian changed my life because it gave me the opportunity to discover and read the original works of some of the best writers and poets of all times, from Dante to Leopardi to Pavese, who shaped my way of seeing the world and of thinking about life.

Being my mothertongue, Italian also gave me a vocabulary to express my deepest feelings and emotions, from love to anger, which somehow always lose their intensity and 'feel' different if I try to translate in another language.

English

English is the foreign language that has had the greatest impact on my life, allowing me to realise many of my dreams and to find my first full-time job.

My first contact with this language happened through music and my passion for British rock. I started learning English by printing the lyrics of my favourite songs and searching on the dictionary the meaning of every single word.

As I started using the internet more frequently in the early 2000s, knowing English I could interact with people with my same interests living in other countries and and discover websites, books and movies that were not translated in Italian.

Eventually, thanks to English I could study abroad for a year, make friends with people from all over the world, and broaden my horizons. After six months of unemployment due to the financial crisis in Italy, English offered me a future: a job in the UK, in the sector I've always loved. All these experiences brought me to where I am today, moving my very first steps in the world of coding, excited about the opportunities that awaits me in the future.
